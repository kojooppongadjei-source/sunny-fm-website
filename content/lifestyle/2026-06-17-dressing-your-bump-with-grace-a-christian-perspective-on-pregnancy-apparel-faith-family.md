**Your Body is Doing Holy Work**

Psalm 139 reminds us that we are fearfully and wonderfully made — and that includes every stage of our bodies, including the round, stretching, growing season of pregnancy. How you dress during this time is an opportunity to honour what God is doing in and through you. A well-fitting, thoughtfully chosen outfit is not about keeping up with trends. It is about moving through your day with confidence, comfort, and dignity.

**Modesty Does Not Mean Frumpy**

One of the biggest myths Christian women face is that dressing modestly means dressing without style. This could not be further from the truth — and pregnancy is the perfect time to prove it. Flowing midi dresses, wrap styles, well-fitted tunics, and breathable fabrics can all be modest, flattering, and beautiful. The goal is clothing that celebrates the life growing within you without drawing attention in ways that make you uncomfortable.

Look for pieces that grow with you — stretchy waistbands, adjustable ties, and soft fabrics that accommodate your changing shape without clinging. Neutral colours and simple patterns work well across multiple occasions, from church to casual outings to family events.

**Practical Wisdom for the Expectant Mother**

Invest in a few quality basics rather than filling your wardrobe with pieces you will only wear once. A good nursing-friendly dress, a comfortable pair of maternity trousers, and two or three versatile tops can take you through most of your pregnancy with ease.

Comfort matters deeply. An uncomfortable woman is a distracted woman — and during pregnancy, your body deserves to feel supported and at ease. Do not sacrifice comfort for the sake of an outfit. Choose both.

Also consider the practical needs of your lifestyle. If you are active in church ministry, serving, or working, your wardrobe should reflect those demands. Breathable, easy-to-move-in clothing honours both your calling and your body.

**A Word to the Church Community**

Pregnancy is visible — and sometimes that visibility brings unsolicited comments, even from well-meaning members of our church families. Let us be a community that celebrates expectant mothers warmly, that speaks life over their pregnancies, and that creates spaces where women feel seen, valued, and beautiful at every stage of their journey.

If you know a pregnant woman in your congregation, encourage her. Celebrate the life she is carrying. Remind her that she is glowing — because she is.
