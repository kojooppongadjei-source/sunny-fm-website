**Faith in the Home**

The home is where faith is either lived or merely professed. It is easy to sing worship songs on Sunday and raise your voice in frustration at home on Monday. The gap between those two moments is where real discipleship happens. For parents especially, the daily rhythms of family life — mealtimes, bedtimes, disagreements, reconciliations — are sacred opportunities to model what it looks like to walk with God.

Colossians 3:17 puts it plainly: *"Whatever you do, whether in word or deed, do it all in the name of the Lord Jesus."* All of it. Not just the obviously spiritual moments.

**Faith at Work**

Many Christians struggle to connect their Monday-to-Friday lives with their Sunday faith. Yet the workplace is one of the most powerful mission fields available to us. Excellence, honesty, generosity, and genuine care for colleagues — these are not just good professional qualities. They are a witness.

You do not need to preach at your desk to live out your faith at work. Show up on time. Do your work with diligence. Treat the cleaner with the same respect you show the director. Let your character speak before your words do.

**Faith in Personal Growth**

Living by faith also means being honest about our struggles — with God and with ourselves. It means pursuing healing, seeking wise counsel, and doing the inner work that makes us more whole. Mental health, physical health, financial stewardship, and personal discipline are all areas where faith has something to say. A life well-lived is not an accident; it is intentional, and that intention is rooted in trusting God with every part of who we are.

**Faith in Relationships**

Friendships, marriages, and community bonds are all strengthened or weakened by the daily choices we make. Forgiveness is not a feeling — it is a decision, and it is one faith calls us to make repeatedly. So is choosing kindness when we would rather be right, or showing up for someone when it costs us something.

In this Lifestyle section, Sunny 88.7 FM will be sharing practical wisdom on living out your faith in the everyday — parenting, relationships, work, health, and personal growth, all from a Christian perspective rooted in grace, truth, and real life.

Because faith was never meant to be something you do. It is meant to be something you are — seven days a week.
