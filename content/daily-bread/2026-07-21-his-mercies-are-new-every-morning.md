There is something deeply comforting about the word "new." Yesterday's failures, yesterday's regrets, yesterday's disappointments — none of them get the final word over today.

Jeremiah wrote these words from the middle of unimaginable loss, watching Jerusalem fall. And yet, in the middle of that devastation, he chose to remember one truth that steadied him: God's mercies are new every morning.

Not renewed once a year. Not restored after we've proven ourselves. New — every single morning, regardless of how we performed the day before.

Maybe you woke up today carrying yesterday's weight — a mistake you can't undo, a conversation that didn't go the way you hoped, a habit you promised yourself you'd break and didn't. Here is your reminder: God is not holding yesterday against you. His mercy already met you before your feet hit the floor this morning.

Great is His faithfulness. Not just when things are going well — but especially when they're not.

Walk into today lighter. His mercy already got here first.
