Psalm 46 was written in the middle of chaos — nations in uproar, mountains shaking, the earth giving way. It's not a psalm about a peaceful season. And it's exactly there, in the middle of the shaking, that God says: be still.

That's not a small ask. Stillness feels almost irresponsible when everything around you is moving fast and demanding a response. Our instinct in chaos is to do something — fix it, control it, figure it out. But this verse suggests that stillness isn't the absence of action; it's an act of trust. It's choosing to stop long enough to remember who's actually in control.

"Know that I am God" isn't just a statement — read in context, it reads almost like a challenge to every other force claiming authority over your life right now: your anxiety, your circumstances, the situation that feels bigger than you. None of it is God. Only God is God.

You don't have to solve everything today. You're invited, even in the middle of the shaking, to be still — and let that stillness remind you who's actually holding it all together.
