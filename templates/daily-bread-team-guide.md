# Sunny Daily Bread — How to Add a New Entry

Two ways to add an entry, depending on what's easiest for you.

## Option A — Through the website admin (easiest, no tech skills needed)

1. Go to **sunnygh.com/admin**
2. Log in
3. Click **Sunny Daily Bread** in the left sidebar
4. Click **New Daily Bread Entry**
5. Fill in the fields (see guide below)
6. Click **Save**, then **Publish**

That's it — the site rebuilds automatically and the new entry goes live.

## Option B — Editing the file directly (for Kojo / dev use)

Copy `templates/daily-bread-template.md` into `content/daily-bread/`, rename it
`YYYY-MM-DD-short-title-slug.md`, fill it in, commit, and push to `main`.

---

## What goes in each field

**Title**
A short theme for the day — not the full verse, just the idea.
✅ "His Mercies Are New Every Morning"
❌ "Devotional for July 21st"

**Publish Date**
The day this should go live. Pick the morning time (6:00 AM works well).

**Scripture Reference**
Book, chapter, verse. e.g. `Lamentations 3:22-23`

**Scripture Text**
The actual verse(s), typed out in full. No quotation marks needed — the
site adds those automatically.

**One-line Summary**
One sentence, plain language, that captures the takeaway. This is what
shows on the archive page under the title, so make it count.
✅ "No matter how yesterday ended, God's mercy meets you fresh this morning."
❌ "A reflection on Lamentations 3:22-23."

**Reflection (the main body)**
3-5 short paragraphs. This is the heart of the entry. A simple structure
that works well:
1. Open with the verse or its context — where it comes from, why it matters
2. Connect it to something real — a feeling, a situation, a struggle
   someone reading might actually be in today
3. Land on encouragement — what does this verse actually offer the reader?

Keep sentences short. Write like you're talking to one person, not a
crowd. Avoid long theological explanations — this is a daily encouragement,
not a sermon.

**Prayer**
2-4 sentences, written as a prayer someone could say for themselves after
reading. Keep it personal ("Lord, help me..." not "We ask that You would...").

**Image (optional)**
Not required — the entry looks good without one. If you have a relevant
photo (sunrise, nature, a relevant scene), it adds warmth, but never let
a missing image hold up publishing.

---

## A few reminders

- **One idea per entry.** Don't try to cover three scriptures — pick one
  verse and go deep, not wide.
- **Write for someone having a hard morning.** That's usually your actual
  reader — someone who needs a reason to keep going today, not a Bible
  study.
- **It's OK to sit with the verse for a day before writing.** The best
  entries usually come from something that actually struck the writer
  first.
- **Consistency matters more than length.** A short entry published every
  day beats a long one published occasionally — the archive builds value
  over time specifically because it's daily.
